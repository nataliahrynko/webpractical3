First Adventure

Adventures at the Algonquin Wildlife Research Station

I met my partner at the AWRs while working on the Small Mammal Project. At one point in the summer during a day off, we treked to the infamous (among past researchers) peak Baldy. It was a lot of pulling the canoe over rocks, hacking at alder through small deep creeks and a constant struggle to not get eaten alive by bugs but by 3pm we had made it to the top of the 550m peak. Lots of fun and a trip I look back at fondly. The map shows the canoe route in blue and the hike up the steep 200m cliff to the oak covered peak (oak is not as common as one would think in the park).
